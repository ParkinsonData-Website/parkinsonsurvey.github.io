Key Information

Provided is a short summary of this study to help you decide whether you want to participate. More detailed information is listed later in this form.

Purpose of the Research

Over 8.5 million individuals worldwide are estimated to be affected by Parkinson’s Disease, and it is the second most common neurodegenerative disease in individuals over 60, after Alzheimer’s. This research project aims to test whether Parkinson’s disease (PD) can be diagnosed by looking at differences in typing rhythm and mouse movements between PD-diagnosed compared to healthy individuals. We are asking you to take a simple online test, and we will use the data collected to train artificial intelligence to distinguish individuals with PD.

Study Procedure

You will be asked to open a website using a specific site link, and complete a few tests using a mouse and keyboard. We will record your hand movements using a keyboard and/or a mouse.

Duration of Participation

Your participation in the study is expected to take about 10 minutes, and it can be done in your own home at a time convenient for you.

Risks and Discomforts

There are no foreseeable risks or discomforts involved in the participation of this study.

Benefits to Participants

While direct benefits to you are limited, your involvement will contribute to the creation of more convenient methods for Parkinson’s disease diagnosis and symptom monitoring, which will improve quality of life for future PD patients.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation in this research is voluntary. You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits.

Criteria for Participation

Participants in the Non-Parkinson’s disease group must be above 50 years of age. Participants in the Parkinson’s disease group must have proof of diagnosis and be above 50 years of age.

Data Sharing

Minimal data will be recorded and shared from this study, including only age, gender, and test data.

Taking part is voluntary

Taking part in this study is voluntary, and the participant may discontinue at any time with no penalty.
